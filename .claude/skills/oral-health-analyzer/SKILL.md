# Oral Health Analysis Skill

## Skill Overview

This skill provides comprehensive oral health data analysis capabilities, including trend identification, risk assessment, problem diagnosis, and personalized recommendation generation.

## Medical Disclaimer

⚠️ **Important**: The data analysis and recommendations provided by this skill are for reference only and do not constitute medical diagnosis or treatment advice.

- All oral health problems should be diagnosed and treated by a qualified dentist
- Analysis results cannot replace a professional oral examination
- Emergencies require immediate medical attention
- Always follow your dentist's professional advice

## Core Functions

### 1. Trend Analysis

#### Cavity Development Trends
- Identify patterns and frequency of cavity occurrence
- Analyze cavity distribution across different tooth positions
- Assess the rate of cavity progression
- Predict future cavity risk

**Output**:
- Cavity count change curve
- High-risk tooth position identification
- Development trend prediction
- Prevention recommendations

#### Periodontal Health Changes
- Periodontal bleeding frequency statistics
- Periodontal pocket depth changes
- Attachment loss monitoring
- Gingival recession progression

**Output**:
- Periodontal health score trends
- Disease progression alerts
- Treatment effectiveness assessment
- Maintenance recommendations

#### Oral Hygiene Habit Improvement
- Brushing frequency changes
- Flossing frequency changes
- Professional cleaning record tracking
- Hygiene habit scores

**Output**:
- Habit improvement curve
- Score change trends
- Goal achievement status
- Motivational recommendations

### 2. Risk Assessment

#### Cavity Risk Assessment
Comprehensive assessment based on the following factors:
- Dietary habits (sugar intake)
- Oral hygiene habits
- Fluoride use
- Saliva secretion status
- Personal cavity history
- Family history

**Risk Levels**:
- **Low Risk**: Good hygiene habits + low-sugar diet + regular check-ups
- **Moderate Risk**: Moderate sugar intake + average hygiene habits
- **High Risk**: High-sugar diet + poor hygiene + irregular check-ups + cavity history

**Output**:
- Risk level (Low/Moderate/High)
- Primary risk factors
- Quantified risk score
- Risk reduction recommendations

#### Periodontal Disease Risk Assessment
Comprehensive assessment based on the following factors:
- Gingival bleeding frequency
- Periodontal pocket depth
- Degree of attachment loss
- Smoking status
- Diabetes control
- Stress level
- Family history

**Risk Levels**:
- **Healthy**: No bleeding, probing depth 1–3mm
- **Gingivitis**: Bleeding on probing, probing depth 3–4mm
- **Mild Periodontitis**: Probing depth 4–5mm, mild attachment loss
- **Moderate Periodontitis**: Probing depth 5–6mm, moderate attachment loss
- **Severe Periodontitis**: Probing depth >6mm, severe attachment loss

**Output**:
- Disease staging
- List of risk factors
- Progression risk prediction
- Management recommendations

#### Oral Cancer Risk Assessment
Comprehensive assessment based on the following factors:
- Smoking history
- Alcohol use habits
- Betel nut chewing
- HPV infection
- Sun exposure (lip cancer)
- Nutritional status
- Oral hygiene

**Risk Levels**:
- **Low Risk**: No risk factors
- **Moderate Risk**: 1–2 risk factors
- **High Risk**: 3 or more risk factors or prior lesions

**Output**:
- Risk level
- Primary risk factors
- Screening recommendations
- Prevention strategies

### 3. Correlation Analysis

#### Correlation with Nutrition Module
**Sugar Intake and Cavity Risk**:
- Analyze daily sugar intake
- Assess the impact of meal frequency on cavities
- Identify high-sugar food types
- Recommend low-sugar food alternatives

**Calcium and Vitamin D and Dental Health**:
- Assess adequacy of calcium intake
- Analyze vitamin D levels
- Evaluate impact on tooth strength
- Recommend supplements (if needed)

**Oral Manifestations of Nutritional Deficiencies**:
- Vitamin C deficiency: gingival bleeding
- Vitamin B deficiency: oral ulcers
- Iron deficiency: tongue inflammation
- Protein deficiency: mucosal atrophy

#### Correlation with Chronic Disease Module
**Diabetes and Periodontal Disease**:
- Analyze the relationship between blood glucose control and periodontal health
- Assess risk of diabetic complications
- Explain the impact of periodontal disease on blood glucose
- Provide joint management recommendations

**Cardiovascular Disease and Periodontal Disease**:
- Analyze the effect of periodontitis on cardiovascular disease
- Assess correlations with inflammatory markers
- Provide preventive treatment recommendations
- Joint monitoring recommendations

**Oral Health During Pregnancy**:
- Pregnancy gingivitis risk assessment
- Dental treatment timing recommendations
- Medication safety assessment
- Oral care guidance during pregnancy

**Osteoporosis and Dental Health**:
- Assess the impact of bone density on teeth
- Analyze side effects of anti-resorptive medications
- Provide tooth protection recommendations

#### Correlation with Medication Module
**Drug-Induced Dry Mouth**:
- Identify medications that cause dry mouth
- Assess severity of dry mouth
- Provide relief recommendations
- Communicate with physician about medication adjustment

**Drug-Induced Gingival Hyperplasia**:
- Identify medications that cause gingival hyperplasia
- Assess degree of hyperplasia
- Provide management recommendations
- Communicate with physician about alternative medications

**Drug Effects on Tooth Color**:
- Identify medications that cause tooth discoloration
- Provide cosmetic solutions
- Preventive measure recommendations

#### Correlation with Eye Health Module
**Sjögren's Syndrome**:
- Joint analysis of dry mouth and dry eyes
- Assess systemic autoimmune disease
- Multi-system symptom tracking
- Specialist referral recommendations

**Oral Manifestations of Autoimmune Diseases**:
- Lupus oral lesions
- Rheumatoid arthritis effects on the temporomandibular joint
- Oral manifestations of other immune conditions

### 4. Personalized Recommendations

#### Prevention Recommendations
**Cavity Prevention**:
- Brushing technique guidance (Bass brushing technique)
- Flossing technique
- Fluoride product recommendations
- Dietary adjustment recommendations
- Regular check-up reminders

**Periodontal Disease Prevention**:
- Improve oral hygiene habits
- Smoking cessation support
- Stress management
- Blood glucose control (for diabetic patients)
- Regular professional cleaning recommendations

**Oral Cancer Prevention**:
- Quit smoking and limit alcohol
- Avoid betel nut
- Sun protection (lips)
- Balanced nutrition
- Regular self-examination methods

#### Treatment Recommendations
**Provided based on problem type**:
- Routine check-up recommendations (every 6 months)
- Emergency situation handling guidance
- Specialist referral recommendations (if needed)
- Treatment timing recommendations
- Estimated cost reference

#### Lifestyle Recommendations
**Dietary Adjustments**:
- Reduce free sugar intake
- Increase calcium and vitamin D intake
- Drink more water (to prevent dry mouth)
- Avoid very hard foods (to protect dental crowns)

**Habit Improvement**:
- Develop personalized brushing plans
- Gradually increase flossing frequency
- Establish oral hygiene routines
- Set up reminder systems

**Risk Factor Management**:
- Smoking cessation strategies
- Alcohol reduction recommendations
- Stress management techniques
- Night bruxism management

### 5. Goal Management

#### Goal Setting
- Collaboratively set realistic goals with the user
- Break down into achievable steps
- Set time milestones
- Establish evaluation criteria

**Common Goal Types**:
- Increase flossing frequency
- Improve brushing technique
- Reduce sugar intake
- Regular oral check-ups
- Quit smoking

#### Progress Tracking
- Regularly assess goal achievement
- Provide motivation and feedback
- Adjust goals (if needed)
- Celebrate milestone achievements

#### Barrier Identification
- Identify factors hindering goal achievement
- Provide strategies to overcome barriers
- Adjust plans to fit actual circumstances
- Provide ongoing support

### 6. Statistical Analysis

#### Comprehensive Health Score
Calculated based on the following factors:
- Oral hygiene habits (40%)
- Check-up frequency (20%)
- Treatment completion (20%)
- Problem control status (10%)
- Goal achievement (10%)

**Score Range**: 0–100
- **Excellent**: 90–100
- **Good**: 75–89
- **Fair**: 60–74
- **Poor**: <60

#### Oral Health Age
- Calculated based on tooth condition, periodontal health, and hygiene habits
- Compared to actual age
- Improvement recommendations provided

#### Treatment Statistics
- Treatment type distribution
- Treatment cost statistics
- Treatment frequency analysis
- Dentist visit records

#### Problem Statistics
- Problem type distribution
- Problem occurrence frequency
- Problem duration
- Resolution rate statistics

### 7. Alert System

#### Regular Check-up Reminders
- 30 days before next check-up: gentle reminder
- 7 days before next check-up: urgent reminder
- Past check-up date: overdue reminder

#### Problem Alerts
- Toothache lasting more than 3 days: recommend seeing a doctor
- Gum bleeding persisting for 1 week: recommend examination
- Oral ulcer lasting more than 2 weeks: recommend biopsy
- New lump/white patch: seek immediate medical attention

#### Trend Alerts
- Rapid increase in number of cavities: risk escalation
- Deteriorating periodontal indicators: referral to periodontal specialist
- Declining hygiene habits: intervention recommendations
- Increasing treatment frequency: in-depth assessment

## Use Cases

### Case 1: Routine Health Assessment
**User Request**: Analyze oral health status over the past 6 months

**Analysis Process**:
1. Read all oral health records from the past 6 months
2. Analyze examination records, treatment records, and problem records
3. Assess changes in hygiene habits
4. Calculate health score changes
5. Identify improving or worsening trends
6. Generate a comprehensive assessment report

**Output**:
- Health score change trends
- Key areas of improvement
- Issues requiring attention
- Next-step action recommendations

### Case 2: Problem Diagnosis Assistance
**User Request**: My gums have been bleeding when I brush my teeth for the past week

**Analysis Process**:
1. Retrieve recent oral examination records
2. Analyze periodontal status history
3. Assess current hygiene habits
4. Check for related medication records
5. Analyze nutritional data (e.g., vitamin C intake)
6. Generate diagnostic assistance report

**Output**:
- Analysis of possible causes
- Severity assessment
- Medical consultation recommendations
- Home care methods
- Preventive measures

### Case 3: Treatment Planning
**User Request**: I want to improve my oral hygiene and reduce cavity risk

**Analysis Process**:
1. Assess current cavity risk
2. Analyze primary risk factors
3. Evaluate current hygiene habits
4. Identify areas needing improvement
5. Set staged goals
6. Develop a personalized plan

**Output**:
- Current risk assessment
- Improvement goals
- Action plan
- Timeline
- Progress tracking methods

### Case 4: Multidisciplinary Joint Analysis
**User Request**: I have diabetes — how does it affect my oral health?

**Analysis Process**:
1. Read diabetes management data
2. Analyze blood glucose control status
3. Assess periodontal health status
4. Analyze the correlation between the two
5. Assess complication risks
6. Generate joint management recommendations

**Output**:
- Impact of diabetes on oral health
- Impact of oral health on blood glucose
- Complication risk assessment
- Joint management strategies
- Monitoring indicator recommendations

### Case 5: Preventive Guidance
**User Request**: I'm planning to become pregnant — what oral issues should I pay attention to?

**Analysis Process**:
1. Assess current oral health status
2. Identify potential risks
3. Analyze current medication safety
4. Assess treatment urgency
5. Generate a prenatal oral management plan

**Output**:
- Pre-pregnancy oral examination recommendations
- Common oral problems during pregnancy
- Medication safety
- Treatment timing recommendations
- Oral care guidance during pregnancy

## Limitations

### System Limitations
- Cannot replace a professional oral examination
- Cannot perform radiographic examination
- Cannot perform laboratory testing
- Analysis results are affected by data quality

### Data Limitations
- Relies on accuracy of user records
- Records may be incomplete
- Subjective assessments may be biased
- Time span may be insufficient

### Recommendation Limitations
- Cannot account for all individual factors
- Cannot predict all complications
- Requires integration with clinical judgment
- Cannot guarantee 100% accuracy
