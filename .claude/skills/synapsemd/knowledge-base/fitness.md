## Exercise Recommendations

### Activity Level Recommendations

**World Health Organization (WHO) Recommendations**:
- Adults (18-64 years): At least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week, or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic exercise
- Older adults (65+ years): At least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity per week, plus strength training 2 or more days per week
- Children/Adolescents: At least 60 minutes of moderate to vigorous-intensity exercise daily

**Daily Activity Recommendations**:
- Daily steps: 8,000-10,000 steps
- Active time: At least 30 minutes of moderate-intensity activity daily
- Sedentary time: Stand and move for 5 minutes every hour

### Exercise Intensity Assessment

**Subjective Feeling Method (RPE)**:
- Light: 1-3 points, can speak easily
- Moderate: 4-6 points, can speak in sentences but cannot sing
- Vigorous: 7-8 points, can only speak a few words
- Maximum: 9-10 points, unable to speak

**Heart Rate Zones**:
- Light: 50-60% of maximum heart rate
- Moderate: 60-70% of maximum heart rate
- Vigorous: 70-85% of maximum heart rate
- Maximum: 85-100% of maximum heart rate

**Maximum Heart Rate Estimation**:
- Simplified formula: Maximum HR = 220 - age
- More accurate: Maximum HR = 208 - (0.7 × age)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Basic Exercise Knowledge

**Q: How many steps should I walk daily?**
A: Generally recommended 8,000-10,000 steps. For health improvement, 7,000 steps provides benefits; for weight management, 10,000+ steps is recommended.

**Q: What is aerobic exercise?**
A: Aerobic exercise refers to large muscle group, rhythmic, continuous exercise, such as walking, running, swimming, cycling, etc.

**Q: How to start exercising?**
A: Progress gradually:
1. Start with light activities (like walking)
2. Gradually increase time and intensity
3. Choose activities you enjoy
4. Set achievable goals

### Exercise Safety

**Q: Do I need to warm up before exercise?**
A: Yes. Warm-up helps:
- Increase muscle temperature
- Improve joint flexibility
- Prevent exercise injuries
- Enhance exercise performance

5-10 minutes of dynamic warm-up is recommended.

**Q: Do I need to stretch after exercise?**
A: Yes. Stretching helps:
- Reduce muscle stiffness
- Improve flexibility
- Promote recovery
- Reduce delayed muscle soreness

5-10 minutes of static stretching is recommended.

### Special Populations

**Q: Can patients with chronic diseases exercise?**
A: Most patients with chronic diseases can benefit from exercise, but need to:
1. Consult a doctor
2. Choose appropriate exercise type and intensity
3. Start with low intensity
4. Monitor physical response
5. Regularly evaluate and adjust

**Q: How can older adults exercise safely?**
A: Older adults should:
1. Choose low-impact activities (walking, swimming)
2. Include balance training to prevent falls
3. Perform strength training to maintain muscle
4. Pay attention to warm-up and stretching
5. Avoid extreme environments

## Exercise and Chronic Diseases

### Hypertension

**Recommended Exercise**:
- Aerobic exercise (walking, cycling, swimming)
- Moderate intensity, 150 minutes per week
- Spread over 5 days per week, 30 minutes per session

**Precautions**:
- Avoid high-intensity training
- Avoid breath-holding movements
- Monitor blood pressure during exercise
- Stop immediately if discomfort occurs

### Diabetes

**Recommended Exercise**:
- Combination of aerobic exercise and strength training
- Best 1-2 hours after meals
- At least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity per week

**Precautions**:
- Monitor blood sugar
- Carry carbohydrate snacks
- Avoid exercising during insulin peak
- Pay attention to foot care

### COPD (Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease)

**Recommended Exercise**:
- Walking, cycling
- Upper limb training
- Breathing exercises

**Precautions**:
- Start with low intensity
- Use bronchodilators
- Learn breathing techniques
- Monitor oxygen saturation
