# Skin Health Analysis Skill

## Skill Overview

This skill provides comprehensive skin health data analysis capabilities, including trend identification, risk assessment, problem diagnosis, and personalized recommendation generation. It places particular emphasis on mole monitoring and skin cancer prevention.

## Medical Disclaimer

⚠️ **Important**: The data analysis and recommendations provided by this skill are for reference only and do not constitute medical diagnosis or treatment advice.

- All skin problems should be diagnosed and treated by a qualified dermatologist
- Abnormal changes in moles must be examined by a doctor immediately
- Skin cancer requires professional diagnosis and cannot rely solely on self-assessment
- Analysis results cannot replace a professional dermatological examination
- Emergencies require immediate medical attention
- Always follow your dermatologist's professional advice

## Core Functions

### 1. Trend Analysis

#### Skin Problem Development Trends
- Identify occurrence patterns of problems such as acne and eczema
- Analyze seasonal and cyclical patterns of problems
- Assess changes in problem severity
- Predict future flare-up risk

**Output**:
- Problem occurrence frequency curve
- Severity change trends
- Trigger factor analysis
- Prevention recommendations

#### Mole Change Monitoring
- Track location and number of new moles
- Monitor size changes in existing moles
- Record ABCDE characteristic changes
- Identify high-risk moles

**Output**:
- Mole distribution map
- Change alert report
- List of moles requiring attention
- Medical consultation recommendations

#### Skincare Effectiveness Assessment
- Analyze skincare routine usage frequency
- Product effectiveness assessment
- Skin condition improvement tracking
- Adverse reaction monitoring

**Output**:
- Skincare effectiveness score
- Product recommendations
- Routine optimization suggestions
- Cost-effectiveness analysis

#### Sun Protection Effectiveness Analysis
- Statistics on sunscreen use
- Frequency of sunburn occurrences
- Photoaging sign assessment
- Sun protection habit improvement recommendations

**Output**:
- Protection score trends
- Risk assessment
- Improvement recommendations
- Product recommendations

### 2. Risk Assessment

#### Skin Cancer Risk Assessment
Comprehensive assessment based on the following factors:
- Skin type (Fitzpatrick classification)
- Sun exposure history
- Number and characteristics of moles
- Sunburn history
- Family history
- History of tanning bed use

**Risk Levels**:
- **Low Risk**: Darker skin, minimal sun exposure, no mole abnormalities
- **Moderate Risk**: Lighter skin, moderate sun exposure, mole abnormalities present
- **High Risk**: Lighter skin, extensive sun exposure, multiple abnormal moles, family history

**Output**:
- Risk level (Low/Moderate/High)
- Primary risk factors
- Quantified risk score
- Risk reduction strategies
- Screening recommendations

#### Acne Severity Assessment
Comprehensive assessment based on the following factors:
- Acne type (blackheads, whiteheads, inflammatory papules, nodules, cysts)
- Number and distribution of lesions
- Degree of inflammation
- Scarring risk

**Severity Classification**:
- **Mild**: Mainly blackheads and whiteheads, few inflammatory lesions
- **Moderate**: More inflammatory lesions, possible mild scarring
- **Severe**: Nodules and cysts, high scarring risk

**Output**:
- Severity classification
- Primary trigger factor analysis
- Treatment recommendation reference
- Skincare recommendations
- Medical consultation recommendations

#### Allergy Risk Identification
Comprehensive assessment based on the following factors:
- Known allergens
- History of skin sensitivity
- Product use history
- Seasonal allergy patterns
- Family allergy history

**Output**:
- List of allergens
- Risk assessment
- Avoidance recommendations
- Alternative product recommendations

#### Photoaging Risk Prediction
Comprehensive assessment based on the following factors:
- Total cumulative sun exposure
- Sun protection habits
- Skin type
- Age
- Lifestyle

**Output**:
- Photoaging risk level
- Current signs of photoaging
- Prevention recommendations
- Treatment option reference

### 3. Correlation Analysis

#### Correlation with Nutrition Module
**Impact of Nutrients on Skin Health**:
- Vitamin A: skin cell renewal, vision
- Vitamin C: collagen synthesis, antioxidant
- Vitamin E: antioxidant, cell membrane protection
- Omega-3 fatty acids: anti-inflammatory effects
- Zinc: wound healing, sebum control
- Water: skin hydration

**Impact of Foods on Skin Problems**:
- High-sugar foods: worsening of acne
- Dairy products: acne trigger factor for some people
- Spicy foods: worsening of rosacea
- Alcohol: skin dehydration, flushing

**Skin Manifestations of Nutritional Deficiencies**:
- Vitamin A deficiency: dry skin, keratinization
- Vitamin C deficiency: slow wound healing, easy bruising
- Vitamin B deficiency: dermatitis, angular cheilitis
- Iron deficiency: pallor, fragility
- Protein deficiency: loose skin, edema

**Output**:
- Nutritional status assessment
- Deficiency risk identification
- Dietary adjustment recommendations
- Supplement recommendations (if needed)

#### Correlation with Chronic Disease Module
**Diabetes and Skin**:
- Diabetic dermopathy
- Delayed wound healing
- Increased risk of fungal infections
- Acanthosis nigricans
- Necrobiosis lipoidica

**Autoimmune Diseases and Skin**:
- Lupus: butterfly rash, photosensitivity
- Rheumatoid arthritis: rheumatoid nodules, vasculitis
- Psoriatic arthritis: psoriatic skin lesions
- Dermatomyositis: Gottron's papules, heliotrope rash

**Thyroid Disease and Skin**:
- Hyperthyroidism: moist skin, fine hair, onycholysis
- Hypothyroidism: dry skin, coarse hair, edema

**Liver Disease and Skin**:
- Jaundice: yellowing of skin and sclera
- Spider angiomas: vascular spider-shaped lesions
- Palmar erythema: redness of the palms
- Skin pruritus: cholestasis

**Output**:
- Correlation analysis of skin symptoms and diseases
- Complication risk assessment
- Comprehensive management recommendations
- Specialist referral recommendations

#### Correlation with Medication Module
**Drug Rash (Drug Allergy)**:
- Common sensitizing drugs: antibiotics, antiepileptics, NSAIDs
- Rash types: morbilliform, urticaria, fixed drug eruption
- Severe reactions: Stevens-Johnson syndrome

**Photosensitizing Medications**:
- Tetracycline-class antibiotics
- Thiazide diuretics
- NSAIDs
- Certain antipsychotics

**Drug-Induced Pigmentation**:
- Minocycline: blue-gray pigmentation
- Amiodarone: blue-gray pigmentation
- Certain chemotherapy drugs

**Drug-Induced Dry Skin**:
- Retinoids
- Benzodiazepines
- Antihistamines (long-term use)

**Output**:
- Drug risk identification
- Interaction analysis
- Alternative drug recommendations (to be discussed with physician)
- Monitoring recommendations

#### Correlation with Endocrine Module
**Impact of Hormonal Changes on Skin**:
- Puberty: increased androgens, acne
- Pregnancy: hyperpigmentation, stretch marks, skin vascular changes
- Menopause: estrogen decline, dry skin, wrinkles
- Menstrual cycle: cyclical acne flares

**Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)**:
- Acne
- Hirsutism
- Androgenic alopecia
- Acanthosis nigricans

**Cushing's Syndrome**:
- Moon face, buffalo hump
- Thin skin, purple striae
- Acne, hirsutism

**Output**:
- Analysis of hormonal impact on skin
- Cyclical symptom identification
- Management recommendations
- Treatment timing recommendations

### 4. Personalized Recommendations

#### Skincare Routine Optimization
**Customized by Skin Type**:
- Dry skin: enhance moisturization, avoid over-cleansing
- Oily skin: control oil, maintain cleanliness, balance oil and moisture
- Combination skin: zone-specific care — oil control in T-zone, moisturization in U-zone
- Normal skin: maintain current routine, basic care
- Sensitive skin: gentle products, avoid irritants

**Customized by Primary Problem**:
- Acne: cleanse, control oil, reduce inflammation, avoid comedogenic ingredients
- Pigmentation: sun protection, brightening ingredients, antioxidants
- Anti-aging: antioxidants, repair, sun protection
- Sensitive skin: soothing, repair, barrier protection

**Output**:
- Morning skincare routine recommendations
- Evening skincare routine recommendations
- Weekly care recommendations
- Product selection guidance
- Budget range recommendations

#### Lifestyle Adjustments
**Dietary Adjustments**:
- Low glycemic index diet (for acne)
- Anti-inflammatory diet (for eczema, psoriasis)
- Antioxidant foods (for anti-aging)
- Adequate fluid intake

**Sleep Management**:
- Ensure 7–9 hours of sleep
- Regular sleep schedule
- Pre-sleep skincare routine
- Pillow hygiene (for acne)

**Stress Management**:
- Identify stress-triggered skin problems
- Learn relaxation techniques
- Regular exercise
- Pursue hobbies

**Environmental Adjustments**:
- Indoor humidity control (for dry skin)
- Avoid allergens (for sensitive skin)
- Workplace protection (for occupational skin problems)

**Output**:
- Personalized lifestyle recommendations
- Goal setting
- Progress tracking methods
- Motivational mechanisms

#### Prevention Measure Recommendations
**Skin Cancer Prevention**:
- Daily sunscreen (SPF 30+)
- Avoid tanning beds
- Regular skin self-examination
- Protect children from excessive sun exposure
- Early detection of abnormal moles

**Acne Prevention**:
- Proper skin cleansing
- Avoid touching the face
- Clean phones and glasses regularly
- Frequent pillowcase changes
- Non-comedogenic cosmetics

**Eczema Prevention**:
- Keep skin moisturized
- Avoid known triggers
- Use gentle detergents
- Wear cotton clothing
- Control indoor temperature and humidity

**Photoaging Prevention**:
- Year-round sun protection
- Antioxidant skincare products
- No smoking
- Adequate sleep
- Healthy diet

**Output**:
- Targeted prevention strategies
- Priority ranking
- Implementation steps
- Effectiveness assessment methods

#### Product Selection Recommendations
**Ingredient Knowledge**:
- Acne treatment: salicylic acid, benzoyl peroxide, retinoids
- Brightening: vitamin C, niacinamide, arbutin
- Anti-aging: retinol, peptides, hyaluronic acid
- Moisturizing: hyaluronic acid, glycerin, ceramides
- Soothing: aloe vera, centella asiatica, oat

**Product Selection Principles**:
- Choose based on skin type
- Avoid known allergens
- Simpler formulations are safer than complex ones
- Fragrance-free formulations are safer
- Try small/sample sizes first

**Reading Product Labels**:
- Identify comedogenic ingredients
- Identify allergens
- Understand active ingredient concentrations
- Understand product efficacy claims

**Output**:
- Ingredient education
- Product recommendation framework (no specific brands)
- List of ingredients to avoid
- Product trial recommendations

### 6. Statistical Analysis

#### Comprehensive Health Score
Calculated based on the following factors:
- Skin problem control status (30%)
- Skincare habits (25%)
- Sun protection (20%)
- Regular check-ups (15%)
- Goal achievement (10%)

**Score Range**: 0–100
- **Excellent**: 90–100
- **Good**: 75–89
- **Fair**: 60–74
- **Poor**: <60

#### Skin Health Age
- Calculated based on skin condition, problem status, and protection habits
- Compared to actual age
- Improvement recommendations provided

#### Problem Statistics
- Problem type distribution
- Problem occurrence frequency
- Problem duration
- Resolution rate statistics
- Recurrence rate analysis

#### Skincare Statistics
- Skincare routine adherence rate
- Product usage frequency
- Skincare expenditure statistics
- Product replacement frequency
- Adverse reaction statistics

### 7. Alert System

#### Mole Change Alerts
- Abnormal increase in number of new moles
- Rapid enlargement of existing moles
- Abnormal ABCDE characteristics
- Change in color or shape
- Symptom onset (itching, bleeding)

**Alert Levels**:
- **Yellow Alert**: Observation needed; consult physician at next check-up
- **Orange Alert**: Prompt medical attention needed (within 1 week)
- **Red Alert**: Immediate medical attention required

#### Skin Problem Alerts
- Sudden worsening of acne
- Newly appearing severe rash
- Signs of drug reaction
- Signs of infection (redness, swelling, heat, pain)
- Skin manifestations of chronic disease

#### Skincare Alerts
- Product adverse reactions
- Improper skincare routine
- Signs of over-skincare
- Use of expired products
- Product interactions

#### Check-up Reminders
- Regular skin self-examination reminders (monthly)
- Dermatologist check-up reminders (annually)
- Mole monitoring reminders (monthly)
- Sunscreen reapplication reminders

## Use Cases

### Case 1: Routine Health Assessment
**User Request**: Analyze skin health status over the past 6 months

**Analysis Process**:
1. Read all skin health records from the past 6 months
2. Analyze problem records, mole monitoring, and skincare records
3. Assess changes in sun protection habits
4. Calculate health score changes
5. Identify improving or worsening trends
6. Generate a comprehensive assessment report

**Output**:
- Health score change trends
- Key areas of improvement
- Issues requiring attention
- Next-step action recommendations

### Case 2: Mole Monitoring Assessment
**User Request**: I noticed a change in a mole on my back — please help me evaluate it

**Analysis Process**:
1. Retrieve historical records for that mole
2. Compare ABCDE characteristic changes
3. Assess risk level
4. Check for other abnormal moles
5. Analyze personal risk factors
6. Generate assessment report

**Output**:
- ABCDE assessment results
- Change severity analysis
- Risk level
- Medical consultation recommendations (strongly recommended/recommended/observation)
- Monitoring frequency recommendations

### Case 3: Acne Management Planning
**User Request**: I want to improve my acne — help me create a management plan

**Analysis Process**:
1. Assess current acne severity

5. Set staged goals
6. Develop a personalized plan

**Output**:
- Current severity assessment
- Primary trigger factor analysis

- When to see a doctor

## Data Analysis Methods

### Quantitative Analysis
- Descriptive statistics (mean, median, standard deviation)
- Trend analysis (linear regression, moving average)
- Correlation analysis (Pearson/Spearman correlation)
- Risk score calculation (multi-factor weighting)
- Time series analysis

### Qualitative Analysis
- Text description analysis
- Symptom pattern recognition
- Chief complaint content classification
- Satisfaction assessment
- Image analysis (if available)

### ABCDE Assessment Algorithm
- Asymmetry score (0–2)
- Border irregularity score (0–2)
- Color uniformity score (0–2)
- Diameter score (0–2)
- Evolution score (0–2)
- Total score ≥4: recommend medical evaluation

## Limitations

### System Limitations

- Cannot perform biopsy

### Data Limitations
- Relies on accuracy of user records
- Records may be incomplete
- Subjective assessments may be biased
- Time span may be insufficient
- Photo quality affects assessment accuracy

### Recommendation Limitations
- Cannot account for all individual factors
- Cannot predict all complications
- Requires integration with clinical judgment
- Cannot guarantee 100% accuracy
- Product recommendations may vary by individual
